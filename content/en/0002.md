Git is a tool called a "version control system" that records the history of changes to files. It's similar to the image of keeping a memo of the entire process of gradually improving a recipe for cooking.

Since it records "when," "what," and "why" something was changed, you can easily revert to a previous recipe later if you want to, and even if multiple people are improving the same recipe at the same time, you can see who changed what.
